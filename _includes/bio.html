Short Bio

I am a cryptographer, engineer, and engineering leader with roughly 15 years of experience in theory and practice of cryptography & distributed systems. Since May 2026, I am on a sabbatical. Prior to that I worked as a Senior Engineering Manager for the DFINITY Foundation, where I helped to design, build, and scale the Internet Computer — from scratch. Before joining DFINITY in 2018, I was a researcher in the crypto group at ISEC (formerly IAIK), Graz University of Technology. My main research interests are in the field of provably secure public key cryptography, with a focus on cryptographically addressing security and privacy issues in modern computing scenarios (e.g., cloud computing or Blockchains) and bringing cutting edge cryptographic schemes and protocols into practice. This also includes work on primitives, conjectured to remain secure in the advent of sufficiently powerful quantum computers. I hold a PhD degree (Dr.techn.) in computer science from Graz University of Technology, which I have obtained in 2017 with distinction.

I am a co-designer of the Picnic family of post-quantum digital signature schemes. Picnic is a third-round candidate in the post-quantum crypto project by NIST. For more information, news, and updates refer to this or to this page.
